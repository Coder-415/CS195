Borscht

Borscht is a soup of Ukranian origin that is made with beetroot as the main ingredient.

Here is an easy recipe for borscht that is ideal for Christmas time.

EASY HOMEMADE BORSCHT

INGREDIENTS

  • 4 cups low or no-sodium vegetable or chicken broth
  • 2 Tbsp neutral oil (like vegetable or grape seed)
  • 1 medium potato
  • 1 cup carrot matchsticks
  • 1 small onion
  • 1/2 tsp. salt
  • 1/4 tsp. coarse black pepper
  • 1 cup frozen green peas
  • 1 cup frozen cut green beans
  • 2 big handfuls of fresh dill
  • 2 (15oz.) cans “original cut” beets
  • 2 tsp. white vinegar
  • 6 Tbsp. whipping cream or sour cream to serve

INSTRUCTIONS

  1. Measure the broth into a large microwave-safe bowl and microwave it for 5 minutes.
  2. Meanwhile, heat the oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat.
  3. Chop the potato into a small (1/4 inch) dice. Add it to the pot.
  4. Increase the heat to medium-high. Stir and then cover the pot.
  5. Roughly chop the carrot matchsticks. Add them to the pot. Stir and cover.
  6. Chop the onion. Add it to the pot. Stir and cover.
  7. When the broth is done heating, add it to the pot along with the salt and pepper.
  8. Increase heat to high, cover and let it come to a simmer. Reduce heat to a moderate simmer.
  9. While the broth is simmering in the pot, put the frozen peas and beans in the microwave-safe bowl and heat until defrosted and warm, about 2 minutes.
  10. While the peas and beans heat, chop the dill. Add half of it to the pot of soup. Put the rest aside for now.
  11. Put a colander over a bowl and pour in the cans of beets. You need the liquid so do not discard it.
  12. Put the drained beets into a food processor and process until they’re in small pieces.
  13. When the potatoes in the pot are tender add the vinegar, the beets, and the beet liquid and the heated peas and beans. Heat on high until simmering.
  14. Stir in the remaining dill. Taste and add more vinegar and black pepper as needed.
  15. Serve with either whipping cream or sour cream on the side for people to add to their bowls of soup.

Mangosteen

Mangosteen is a tropical fruit found in parts of Asia. It has a deep purple outside and a white fleshy grapefruit like inside.

Here is an easy recipe for mangosteen popsicles that uses mangosteen juice and is perfect for a hot summer day.

HEALTHY MANGOSTEEN POPSICLES

Ingredients

  • Popsicle molds
  • Wooden sticks
  • 2 cans full fat coconut milk
  • 2-3 medjool dates (pitted and softened)
  • 2-3 cups Mangosteen Juice
  • 2 tbsp coconut butter

Instructions

  1. To prepare the dates, boil water and pour over dates in a small bowl. Let soften for about 10 minutes.
  2. In the meantime heat coconut milk and coconut butter over low to medium heat. Squeeze out any excess liquid from the dates and add them to the coconut milk. Simmer for about 5-10 minutes until dates are dissolved. Strain through a sieve and pour into a measuring cup.
  3. To prepare the poposicles fill each popsicle mold with 1/3 of mango juice, then fill up the rest with the coconut milk mixture. Place popsicles in freezer for about 30-40 minuts before adding the wooden sticks. Freeze for another 2 hours before serving.
